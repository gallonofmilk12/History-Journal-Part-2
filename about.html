About Our Journal

Learn about our objectives and submission rules

Journal Objectives

The Student History Journal aims to foster academic excellence and provide a platform for history students to showcase their research and analytical skills. Our objectives include:

  • Encouraging original historical research and critical thinking among students
  • Providing a platform for peer review and constructive academic criticism
  • Creating a collection of high-quality student publications on historical topics
  • Building a community of history scholars and enthusiasts
  • Helping students gain experience with academic publishing processes

Submission Rules & Guidelines

Paper Format

  • Papers should be 1,500-5,000 words in length (excluding citations)
  • Use 12pt Times New Roman font, double-spaced
  • Include a title, abstract (200 words max), and keywords
  • Follow Chicago Manual of Style for citations
  • Submit in PDF format

Content Requirements

  • Original research or analysis on historical topics
  • Clear thesis statement and supporting evidence
  • Proper citation of all sources
  • Academic tone and language
  • Factual accuracy and historical context

Peer Review Process

  • All submissions undergo double-blind peer review
  • Papers are evaluated by at least two student reviewers
  • Review criteria: originality, quality of analysis, evidence, and writing
  • Authors will receive feedback and revision requests if applicable
  • Final publication decisions made by editorial board

Submission Timeline

  • Submissions accepted on a rolling basis
  • Initial review completed within 3 weeks
  • Revision period (if needed): 2 weeks
  • Final decision within 1 week of revision submission
  • Publication within 2 weeks of acceptance

Ethical Guidelines

The Student History Journal is committed to maintaining high academic and ethical standards. We expect all authors to adhere to the following principles:

  • Academic honesty and integrity in all research and writing
  • Proper attribution of all sources and ideas
  • Respect for diverse perspectives and interpretations
  • Objectivity in analysis and presentation of historical evidence
  • Responsible handling of sensitive historical subjects

Any submission found to contain plagiarism or other ethical violations will be immediately rejected.
